My Startup experience and further collaboration with Aalto Ventures Programm

From a regular student to an active creator


My first meeting with Aalto Ventures Programm was for the course "Startup experience". It is a practical course on entrepreneurship offered by Aalto Venture Programme (AVP) that I did in the spring term of 2019.


I applied as a CEO with a concrete idea for smart fashion tech solution for people with scoliosis and back pain. As a CEO, I led a team of brilliant people from diverse backgrounds and many hidden talents who liked my idea and were willing to get deeper into back pain for three months.
The twelve weeks course covers different theoretical areas about entrepreneurship and presents useful tools such as Trello, Canvas etc. But the dry theory is not the focus of it: we worked on our practical projects in a team. Especially valuable was the regular clash with reality: we got feedback on our ideas and pitches from professionals from the start-up scene in Helsinki, such as /TBWA, venture capital fund Inventure, student-run Venture capital fund Wave Ventures, and the social change think-tank Demos Helsinki.

Approaching the end of the course I facilitated a workshop session on designing your life together with AVP’s experienced trainer Lidia Borisova. It was the beginning of a fruitful collaboration on the autumn course "Good Life Engine".


Commitment to routines is hard! "Good Life Engine" is a nine-month course that focuses on the practical implementation of well-being routines on a daily basis. The objective is to continiously support the student’s own commitment towards their personal goals.
Together with Lidia Borisova and Johannes Kaira, I worked on the conseptualization of the course and its launch in September 2019.
